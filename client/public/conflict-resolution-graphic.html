Types of Conflict Resolution Techniques

Understanding the spectrum of options available for resolving family disputes — from collaborative negotiation to formal court proceedings — helps you make the most informed choice for your situation.

1 Negotiation
2 Mediation
3 Arbitration
4 Litigation
Step 1 — Most Collaborative
Negotiation
  • Parties work directly together to reach a mutually acceptable settlement
  • Offers the best alternative to formal agreements, preserving relationships
  • Achieves acceptable outcomes for both parties without outside interference
  • Lowest cost and fastest resolution; fully private and confidential
Step 2 — Facilitated
Mediation
  • A professional mediator facilitates discussions and helps settle disagreements
  • Mediator is a neutral third party with no bias toward either side
  • Both internal and external mediators can be used depending on the situation
  • Voluntary, confidential process — parties retain full control of outcomes
Step 3 — Binding Decision
Arbitration
  • Similar to a court trial with an unbiased third party acting as judge
  • Arbitrator listens to both sides and renders a binding decision
  • More formal than mediation but less public than court litigation
  • Can be more expensive and time-consuming than mediation
Step 4 — Formal Court
Litigation
  • Plaintiff and defendant present their case before a judge or jury
  • Civil court proceedings are publicly recorded and accessible
  • Judge issues an official, enforceable decision (often settled during pretrial)
  • Most costly and time-consuming option; least control over outcomes
